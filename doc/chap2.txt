  
  [1X2 [33X[0;0YDescription[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe   group   [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X   is  generated  by  [23X\mathfrak{s}[123X  =
  [10X[[0,1],[-1,0]][110X and [23X\mathfrak{t}[123X = [10X[[1,1],[0,1]][110X (which satisfy the relations
  [23X\mathfrak{s}^4   =  (\mathfrak{st})^3  =  \mathrm{id}[123X).  Thus,  any  complex
  representation  [23X\rho[123X  of  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X on [23X\mathbb{C}^n[123X (where [23Xn
  \in  \mathbb{Z}^+[123X is called the [13Xdegree[113X of [23X\rho[123X) is defined by the [23Xn \times n[123X
  matrices [23XS = \rho(\mathfrak{s})[123X and [23XT = \rho(\mathfrak{t})[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThis    package    constructs    representations    which   factor   through
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/\ell\mathbb{Z})[123X for some [23X\ell \in \mathbb{Z}^+[123X; the
  smallest  such  [23X\ell[123X  is  called  the  [13Xlevel[113X  of the representation. One may
  equivalently  say  that  the  kernel  of  the representation is a congruence
  subgroup.  It  has been shown that any representation arising from a modular
  tensor category has this property [DLN15].[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWe  therefore  present  representations  in  the  form of a record [10Xrec(S, T,
  degree, level, name)[110X where the name follows the conventions of [NW76].[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YNote  that  our  definition  of  [23X\mathfrak{s}[123X follows that of [Nob76]; other
  authors  prefer the inverse, i.e. [23X\mathfrak{s}[123X = [10X[[0,-1],[1,0]][110X (under which
  convention    the    relations    are    [23X\mathfrak{s}^4    =    \mathrm{id}[123X,
  [23X(\mathfrak{s}\mathfrak{t})^3  =  \mathfrak{s}^2[123X).  When  working  with  that
  convention, one must invert the [23XS[123X matrices output by this package.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThroughout,  we  denote  by  [23X\mathbf{e}[123X  the map [23Xk \mapsto e^{2 \pi i k}[123X (an
  isomorphism from [23X\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}[123X to the group of finite roots of unity
  in [23X\mathbb{C}[123X).[133X
  
  
  [1X2.1 [33X[0;0YConstruction[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YAny  representation [23X\rho[123X of [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X can be decomposed into
  a  direct  sum of irreducible representations (irreps). Further, if [23X\rho[123X has
  finite  level,  each irrep can be factorized into a tensor product of irreps
  whose  levels  are  powers  of  distinct primes (using the Chinese remainder
  theorem).    Therefore,    to    characterize    all    representations   of
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X,    it    suffices    to    consider   irreps   of
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})[123X  for  primes  [23Xp[123X  and  positive
  integers [23X\lambda[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YSuch  representations  may  be  constructed  using  Weil  representations as
  described  in  [Nob76,  Section  1].  We give a brief summary of the process
  here.  First, if [23XM[123X is any additive abelian group, a [13Xquadratic form[113X on [23XM[123X is a
  map  [23XQ  : M \to \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}[123X such that [23XQ(-x) = Q(x)[123X for all [23Xx \in M[123X
  and [23XB(x,y) = Q(x+y) - Q(x) - Q(y)[123X defines a [23X\mathbb{Z}[123X-bilinear map [23XM \times
  M \to \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YNow  let  [23Xp[123X  be  a  prime  number  and  [23X\lambda  \in  \mathbb{Z}^+[123X. Choose a
  [23X\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}[123X-module  [23XM[123X of rank 1 or 2 and a quadratic form
  [23XQ[123X  on  [23XM[123X  such that the pair [23X(M,Q)[123X is of one of the three types described in
  Section  [14X2.2[114X. Then the [13Xquadratic module[113X [23X(M,Q)[123X gives rise to a representation
  of  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})[123X  on  the  vector space [23XV =
  \mathbb{C}^M[123X  of  complex-valued  functions  on  [23XM[123X.  This  representation is
  denoted [23XW(M,Q)[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWith    a   finite   number   of   exceptions,   every   representation   of
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})[123X    may    be    found   as   a
  subrepresentation  of  [23XW(M,Q)[123X  for  an  appropriate  choice  of [23X(M,Q)[123X [NW76,
  Hauptsatz  2] (the 18 exceptions can be constructed as the tensor product of
  two    such    subrepresentations;    these    can    be    generated   with
  [2XSL2ExceptionalIrreps[102X ([14X3.3-1[114X)).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  subrepresentations  in  question  are  often  of  the form [23XW(M,Q,\chi)[123X,
  defined as follows. Let [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X be an abelian subgroup of[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\operatorname{Aut}(M,Q)  =  \{  \varepsilon  \in  \operatorname{Aut}(M) \mid
  Q(\varepsilon x) = Q(x) \text{ for all } x \in M\}[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0Yand  let  [23X\chi  \in  \widehat{\mathfrak{A}}[123X  be a 1-dimensional character of
  [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X. Define[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6YV_\chi = \{f \in V \mid f(\varepsilon x) = \chi(\varepsilon) f(x) \text{ for
  all } x \in M \text{ and } \varepsilon \in \mathfrak{A}\}~,[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0Ywhich  is a [23XSL_2(\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})[123X-invariant subspace of [23XV[123X. We
  then denote by [23XW(M,Q,\chi)[123X the subrepresentation of [23XW(M,Q)[123X on [23XV_\chi[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  this  context,  we  will  frequently  refer to a character [23X\chi[123X as being
  [13Xprimitive[113X,  which  means that there exists some [23X\varepsilon \in \mathfrak{A}[123X
  such  that  [23X\chi(\varepsilon)  \neq 1[123X and [23X\varepsilon[123X fixes the submodule [23XpM[123X
  pointwise  (except  for  one  special  case of type [23XR[123X; for this, see Section
  [14X2.2-3[114X). This amounts to saying that [23X\chi[123X does not factor through a quadratic
  module of lower level.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YAll  the  irreducible representations of [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X of finite
  level  can now be constructed by taking tensor products of these prime-power
  irreps.  Note that, if two representations are defined by pairs [10X[S1, T1][110X and
  [10X[S2,  T2][110X, then their tensor product may be calculated using the GAP command
  [10XKroneckerProduct[110X,         namely         as        [10X[KroneckerProduct(S1,S2),
  KroneckerProduct(T1,T2)][110X.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.2 [33X[0;0YWeil representation types[133X[101X
  
  
  [1X2.2-1 [33X[0;0YType D[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet [23Xp[123X be prime and [23X\lambda \geq 1[123X. Then the Weil representation arising from
  the   quadratic   module  with  [23XM  =  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}  \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}[123X  and [23XQ(x,y) = \frac{xy}{p^\lambda}[123X is said to
  be  of type [23XD[123X and denoted [23XD(p,\lambda)[123X. Information on [23X(M,Q)[123X may be obtained
  via  [2XSL2ModuleD[102X  ([14X5.1-1[114X),  and subrepresentations of [23XD(p,\lambda)[123X with level
  [23Xp^\lambda[123X may be constructed via [2XSL2IrrepD[102X ([14X5.1-2[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YHere we define[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\mathfrak{A} = (\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})^\times[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0Yacting  on  [23XM[123X by multiplication; see [NW76, Section 2.1]. This group has the
  following  structure. When [23Xp=2[123X and [23X\lambda \geq 3[123X, it is generated by [23X\alpha
  =  -1[123X  and  [23X\zeta  =  5[123X.  Otherwise, it is cyclic; in this case, we choose a
  generator [23X\alpha[123X and (for simplicity) say [23X\zeta = 1[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YA  character  of [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X is primitive if and only if it is injective on
  the  subgroup  [23X\langle\omicron\rangle  \leq  \mathfrak{A}[123X, where [23X\omicron[123X is
  chosen  as follows. If [23Xp=2[123X and [23X\lambda \geq 3[123X, [23X\omicron = 5[123X. If [23X\lambda = 1[123X,
  [23X\omicron = \alpha[123X. In all other cases, [23X\omicron = 1+p[123X.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.2-2 [33X[0;0YType N[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet [23Xp[123X be prime and [23X\lambda \geq 1[123X. Then the Weil representation arising from
  the   quadratic   module  with  [23XM  =  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}  \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}[123X       and       [23XQ(x,y)       =      \frac{x^2
  +xy+\frac{1+u}{4}y^2}{p^\lambda}[123X (where, for [23Xp \neq 2[123X, [23Xu[123X is chosen so that [23Xu
  \equiv  3[123X  mod  4  with [23X\left(\frac{-u}{p}\right) = -1[123X, and for [23Xp=2[123X, [23Xu=3[123X) is
  said  to  be of type [23XN[123X and denoted [23XN(p,\lambda)[123X. Information on [23X(M,Q)[123X may be
  obtained via [2XSL2ModuleN[102X ([14X5.2-1[114X), and subrepresentations of [23XD(p,\lambda)[123X with
  level [23Xp^\lambda[123X may be constructed via [2XSL2IrrepN[102X ([14X5.2-2[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YHere we define[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\mathfrak{A}       =       \{\varepsilon       \in       M^\times       \mid
  \operatorname{Nm}(\varepsilon) = 1 \}[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0Yacting  on  [23XM[123X by multiplication; see [NW76, Section 2.2]. This group has the
  following  structure.  When  [23X\lambda  \geq  2[123X, it is generated by [23X\alpha[123X and
  [23X\zeta[123X:  for  [23Xp=2[123X, [23X|\alpha| = 2^{\lambda-2}[123X and [23X|\zeta| = 6[123X, while for [23Xp \neq
  2[123X,  [23X|\alpha|  =  p^{\lambda-1}[123X  and  [23X|\zeta|  = p+1[123X. On the other hand, when
  [23X\lambda  =  1[123X,  [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X  is cyclic; in this case, we choose a generator
  [23X\zeta[123X with [23X|\zeta| = p+1[123X and (for simplicity) say [23X\alpha = 1[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YAs  a  special  case,  for  [23Xp=2[123X,  [23X\lambda=2[123X,  a character of [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X is
  primitive  if and only if [23X\chi(-1) = -1[123X. For all other cases, a character of
  [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X  is  primitive  if  and only if it is injective on the subgroup
  [23X\langle\omicron\rangle  \leq  \mathfrak{A}[123X,  where  [23X\omicron[123X  is  chosen  as
  follows. If [23X\lambda = 1[123X, [23X\omicron = \zeta[123X; otherwise [23X\omicron = \alpha[123X.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.2-3 [33X[0;0YType R[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  structure  of [23X(M,Q)[123X of type [23XR[123X depends upon three additional parameters:
  [23X\sigma[123X,  [23Xr[123X,  and  [23Xt[123X.  The  relevant values thereof depend on whether [23Xp=2[123X, as
  follows.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YFirst,  if  [23Xp[123X  is  an  odd prime, let [23X\lambda \geq 2[123X, [23X\sigma \in \{1, \dots,
  \lambda\}[123X,  and  [23Xr,t  \in \{1,u\}[123X with [23Xu[123X a quadratic non-residue mod [23Xp[123X. Then
  define        [23XM        =        \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}        \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/p^{\lambda-\sigma}\mathbb{Z}[123X  and [23XQ(x,y) = \frac{r(x^2 + p^\sigma
  t y^2)}{p^\lambda}[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YOn  the  other  hand,  if  [23Xp=2[123X,  let  [23X\lambda \geq 2[123X, [23X\sigma \in \{0, \dots,
  \lambda-2\}[123X    and    [23Xr,t    \in    \{1,3,5,7\}[123X.    Then    define    [23XM    =
  \mathbb{Z}/2^{\lambda-1}\mathbb{Z}                                    \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/2^{\lambda-\sigma-1}\mathbb{Z}[123X   and   [23XQ(x,y)   =  \frac{r(x^2  +
  2^\sigma t y^2)}{2^\lambda}[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  either  case,  the  resulting representation is said to be of type [23XR[123X and
  denoted  [23XR(p,\lambda,\sigma,r,t)[123X.  Information  on [23X(M,Q)[123X may be obtained via
  [2XSL2ModuleR[102X  ([14X5.3-1[114X),  and subrepresentations of [23XR(p,\lambda,\sigma,r,t)[123X with
  level  [23Xp^\lambda[123X  may  be  constructed  via  [2XSL2IrrepR[102X ([14X5.3-2[114X). Note that if
  [23X\sigma  =  \lambda[123X for [23Xp \neq 2[123X, then the second factor of [23XM[123X is trivial (and
  hence  [23Xt[123X  is  irrelevant);  this  special  case is handled by [2XSL2IrrepRUnary[102X
  ([14X5.3-3[114X).[133X
