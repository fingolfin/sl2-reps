  
  [1X2 [33X[0;0YDescription[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe   group   [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X   is  generated  by  [23X\mathfrak{s}[123X  =
  [10X[[0,1],[-1,0]][110X and [23X\mathfrak{t}[123X = [10X[[1,1],[0,1]][110X (which satisfy the relations
  [23X\mathfrak{s}^4   =  (\mathfrak{st})^3  =  \mathrm{id}[123X).  Thus,  any  complex
  representation  [23X\rho[123X  of  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X on [23X\mathbb{C}^n[123X (where [23Xn
  \in \mathbb{Z}^+[123X is called the [13Xdegree[113X of [23X\rho[123X) is determined by the [23Xn \times
  n[123X matrices [23XS = \rho(\mathfrak{s})[123X and [23XT = \rho(\mathfrak{t})[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThis      package      constructs     irreducible     representations     of
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X           which           factor           through
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/\ell\mathbb{Z})[123X for some [23X\ell \in \mathbb{Z}^+[123X; the
  smallest  such  [23X\ell[123X  is  called  the  [13Xlevel[113X  of the representation. One may
  equivalently  say  that  the  kernel  of  the representation is a congruence
  subgroup.    It    has    been    shown    that    any   representation   of
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X  arising  from  a modular tensor category has this
  property [DLN15].[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWe  therefore  present  representations  in  the  form of a record [10Xrec(S, T,
  degree, level, name)[110X, where the name follows the conventions of [NW76].[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YNote  that  our  definition  of  [23X\mathfrak{s}[123X follows that of [Nob76]; other
  authors  prefer the inverse, i.e. [23X\mathfrak{s}[123X = [10X[[0,-1],[1,0]][110X (under which
  convention    the    relations    are    [23X\mathfrak{s}^4    =    \mathrm{id}[123X,
  [23X(\mathfrak{s}\mathfrak{t})^3  =  \mathfrak{s}^2[123X).  When  working  with  that
  convention, one must invert the [23XS[123X matrices output by this package.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThroughout,  we  denote  by  [23X\mathbf{e}[123X  the map [23Xk \mapsto e^{2 \pi i k}[123X (an
  isomorphism from [23X\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}[123X to the group of finite roots of unity
  in [23X\mathbb{C}[123X).[133X
  
  
  [1X2.1 [33X[0;0YConstruction[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YAny  representation [23X\rho[123X of [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X can be decomposed into
  a  direct  sum of irreducible representations (irreps). Further, if [23X\rho[123X has
  finite  level,  each irrep can be factorized into a tensor product of irreps
  whose  levels  are  powers  of  distinct primes (using the Chinese remainder
  theorem).  Therefore, to characterize all finite-dimensional representations
  of [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X of finite level, it suffices to consider irreps
  of  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})[123X  for primes [23Xp[123X and positive
  integers [23X\lambda[123X.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.1-1 [33X[0;0YWeil representations[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YSuch  representations  may  be  constructed  using  Weil  representations as
  described  in  [Nob76,  Section  1].  We give a brief summary of the process
  here.  First, if [23XM[123X is any additive abelian group, a [13Xquadratic form[113X on [23XM[123X is a
  map  [23XQ  : M \to \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}[123X such that [23XQ(-x) = Q(x)[123X for all [23Xx \in M[123X
  and [23XB(x,y) = Q(x+y) - Q(x) - Q(y)[123X defines a [23X\mathbb{Z}[123X-bilinear map [23XM \times
  M \to \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YNow  let  [23Xp[123X  be  a  prime  number  and  [23X\lambda  \in  \mathbb{Z}^+[123X. Choose a
  [23X\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}[123X-module  [23XM[123X  and  a  quadratic form [23XQ[123X on [23XM[123X such
  that  the  pair [23X(M,Q)[123X is of one of the three types described in Section [14X2.2[114X.
  Each  such  [23XM[123X  is  a  ring,  and has at most 2 cyclic factors as an additive
  group.  Those with 2 cyclic factors may be identified with a quotient of the
  quadratic  integers,  giving  a  norm  on [23XM[123X. Then the [13Xquadratic module[113X [23X(M,Q)[123X
  gives          rise          to          a         representation         of
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})[123X   on  the  vector  space  [23XV  =
  \mathbb{C}^M[123X  of  complex-valued  functions  on  [23XM[123X.  This  representation is
  denoted [23XW(M,Q)[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWe may construct subrepresentations [23XW(M,Q,\chi)[123X of [23XW(M,Q)[123X as follows. Let[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\operatorname{Aut}(M,Q)  =  \{  \varepsilon  \in  \operatorname{Aut}(M) \mid
  Q(\varepsilon x) = Q(x) \text{ for all } x \in M\}[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0Yand denote by [23X\mathfrak{A} \leq \operatorname{Aut}(M,Q)[123X the abelian subgroup
  defined  in  Section  [14X2.2[114X.  Note  that  [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X  has at most two cyclic
  factors,  whose generators are usually denoted by [23X\alpha[123X and [23X\beta[123X. Let [23X\chi
  \in  \widehat{\mathfrak{A}}[123X be a 1-dimensional representation (character) of
  [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X, and define[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6YV_\chi = \{f \in V \mid f(\varepsilon x) = \chi(\varepsilon) f(x) \text{ for
  all } x \in M \text{ and } \varepsilon \in \mathfrak{A}\}~,[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0Ywhich  is a [23XSL_2(\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})[123X-invariant subspace of [23XV[123X. We
  then denote by [23XW(M,Q,\chi)[123X the subrepresentation of [23XW(M,Q)[123X on [23XV_\chi[123X.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.1-2 [33X[0;0YPrimitive characters[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YWe  will  frequently  refer to a character [23X\chi[123X as being [13Xprimitive[113X. With the
  exception  of  a  single family of modules of type [23XR[123X (the [13Xextremal[113X case, for
  which  see Section [14X2.2-3[114X) primitivity amounts to the following: there exists
  some  [23X\varepsilon  \in  \mathfrak{A}[123X  such that [23X\chi(\varepsilon) \neq 1[123X and
  [23X\varepsilon[123X  fixes  the  submodule  [23XpM[123X  pointwise.  In  the  extremal  case,
  [23X\mathfrak{A}  =  \langle\alpha\rangle  \times  \langle-1\rangle[123X (see Section
  [14X2.2-3[114X  for  an  explicit  definition  of  [23X\alpha[123X)  and  [23X\chi[123X is primitive if
  [23X\chi(\alpha)  =  -1[123X.  Explicit  descriptions of the primitive characters are
  given in Section [14X2.2[114X.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.1-3 [33X[0;0YIrrep types[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe prime-power irreps then fall into three cases.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YThe  overwhelming  majority  are  of  the  form  [23XW(M,Q,\chi)[123X  for [23X\chi[123X
        primitive;  we  call  these  [13Xstandard[113X.  This  includes  the  primitive
        characters from the extremal case.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YA  finite  number,  and  a  single  infinite  family  arising from the
        extremal  case  (Section  [14X2.2-3[114X),  are  instead  constructed  by using
        non-primitive characters. We call these [13Xnon-standard[113X.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YFinally,  18  [13Xexceptional[113X irreps are constructed as tensor products of
        two irreps from the other two cases.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YAll     the     finite-dimensional     irreducible     representations    of
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X  of  finite level can now be constructed by taking
  tensor   products   of   these   prime-power   irreps.  Note  that,  if  two
  representations  are defined by pairs [10X[S1,T1][110X and [10X[S2,T2][110X, then their tensor
  product  may  be  calculated via the GAP command [10XKroneckerProduct[110X, namely as
  [10X[KroneckerProduct(S1,S2),KroneckerProduct(T1,T2)][110X.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.2 [33X[0;0YWeil representation types[133X[101X
  
  
  [1X2.2-1 [33X[0;0YType D[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet [23Xp[123X be prime and [23X\lambda \geq 1[123X. Then the Weil representation arising from
  the   quadratic   module  with  [23XM  =  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}  \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}[123X  and [23XQ(x,y) = \frac{xy}{p^\lambda}[123X is said to
  be  of type [23XD[123X and denoted [23XD(p,\lambda)[123X. Information on [23X(M,Q)[123X may be obtained
  via  [2XSL2ModuleD[102X  ([14X5.1-1[114X),  and subrepresentations of [23XD(p,\lambda)[123X with level
  [23Xp^\lambda[123X may be constructed via [2XSL2IrrepD[102X ([14X5.1-2[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YHere we define[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\mathfrak{A} \cong (\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})^\times[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0Yacting  on  [23XM[123X by [23Xa(x,y) = (a^{-1}x, ay)[123X; see [NW76, Section 2.1]. This group
  has the following structure. When [23Xp=2[123X and [23X\lambda \geq 3[123X, it is generated by
  [23X\alpha  = 5[123X and [23X\beta = -1[123X. Otherwise, it is cyclic; in this case, we choose
  a generator [23X\alpha[123X and (for simplicity) say [23X\beta = 1[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YFor  type  [23XD[123X,  a character of [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X is primitive if and only if it is
  injective  on  the  subgroup  [23X\langle\omega\rangle  \leq \mathfrak{A}[123X, where
  [23X\omega[123X  is  chosen as follows. If [23X\lambda = 1[123X, or if [23Xp=2[123X and [23X\lambda \geq 3[123X,
  then [23X\omega = \alpha[123X. In all other cases, [23X\omega = 1+p[123X.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.2-2 [33X[0;0YType N[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet  [23Xp[123X  be  prime  and  [23X\lambda \geq 1[123X. If [23Xp \neq 2[123X, choose some [23Xu[123X so that [23Xu
  \equiv  3[123X mod 4 with [23X-u[123X a quadratic non-residue mod [23Xp[123X; if [23Xp = 2[123X, define [23Xu=3[123X.
  Then  the  Weil  representation  arising  from the quadratic module with [23XM =
  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}   \oplus  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}[123X  and
  [23XQ(x,y)  = \frac{x^2 +xy+\frac{1+u}{4}y^2}{p^\lambda}[123X is said to be of type [23XN[123X
  and   denoted  [23XN(p,\lambda)[123X.  Information  on  [23X(M,Q)[123X  may  be  obtained  via
  [2XSL2ModuleN[102X  ([14X5.2-1[114X),  and  subrepresentations  of  [23XN(p,\lambda)[123X  with  level
  [23Xp^\lambda[123X may be constructed via [2XSL2IrrepN[102X ([14X5.2-2[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YHere we define[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\mathfrak{A}       =       \{\varepsilon       \in       M^\times       \mid
  \operatorname{Nm}(\varepsilon) = 1 \}[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0Yacting  on  [23XM[123X by multiplication; see [NW76, Section 2.2]. This group has the
  following  structure.  When  [23X\lambda  \geq  2[123X, it is generated by [23X\alpha[123X and
  [23X\beta[123X:  for  [23Xp=2[123X, [23X|\alpha| = 2^{\lambda-2}[123X and [23X|\beta| = 6[123X, while for [23Xp \neq
  2[123X,  [23X|\alpha|  =  p^{\lambda-1}[123X  and  [23X|\beta|  = p+1[123X. On the other hand, when
  [23X\lambda  =  1[123X,  [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X  is cyclic; in this case, we choose a generator
  [23X\beta[123X with [23X|\beta| = p+1[123X and (for simplicity) say [23X\alpha = 1[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YFor  type [23XN[123X with [23Xp=2[123X, [23X\lambda=2[123X, a character of [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X is primitive if
  and  only  if  [23X\chi(-1)  =  -1[123X.  For  all other type [23XN[123X cases, a character of
  [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X  is  primitive  if  and only if it is injective on the subgroup
  [23X\langle\omega\rangle  \leq  \mathfrak{A}[123X, where [23X\omega[123X is chosen as follows.
  If [23X\lambda = 1[123X, [23X\omega = \beta[123X; otherwise [23X\omega = \alpha[123X.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.2-3 [33X[0;0YType R[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  structure  of [23X(M,Q)[123X of type [23XR[123X depends upon three additional parameters:
  [23X\sigma[123X,  [23Xr[123X,  and  [23Xt[123X.  The  relevant values thereof depend on whether [23Xp=2[123X, as
  follows.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YFirst,  if  [23Xp[123X  is  an  odd prime, let [23X\lambda \geq 2[123X, [23X\sigma \in \{1, \dots,
  \lambda\}[123X,  and  [23Xr,t  \in \{1,u\}[123X with [23Xu[123X a quadratic non-residue mod [23Xp[123X. Then
  define        [23XM        =        \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}        \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/p^{\lambda-\sigma}\mathbb{Z}[123X  and [23XQ(x,y) = \frac{r(x^2 + p^\sigma
  t y^2)}{p^\lambda}[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YOn  the  other  hand,  if  [23Xp=2[123X,  let  [23X\lambda \geq 2[123X, [23X\sigma \in \{0, \dots,
  \lambda-2\}[123X    and    [23Xr,t    \in    \{1,3,5,7\}[123X.    Then    define    [23XM    =
  \mathbb{Z}/2^{\lambda-1}\mathbb{Z}                                    \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/2^{\lambda-\sigma-1}\mathbb{Z}[123X   and   [23XQ(x,y)   =  \frac{r(x^2  +
  2^\sigma t y^2)}{2^\lambda}[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  either  case,  the  resulting representation is said to be of type [23XR[123X and
  denoted  [23XR(p,\lambda,\sigma,r,t)[123X.  Information  on [23X(M,Q)[123X may be obtained via
  [2XSL2ModuleR[102X  ([14X5.3-1[114X),  and subrepresentations of [23XR(p,\lambda,\sigma,r,t)[123X with
  level [23Xp^\lambda[123X may be constructed via [2XSL2IrrepR[102X ([14X5.3-2[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThere  are  two  special cases to consider. First, if [23X\sigma = \lambda[123X for [23Xp
  \neq  2[123X, then the second factor of [23XM[123X is trivial (and hence [23Xt[123X is irrelevant);
  this  special  case is handled by [2XSL2IrrepRUnary[102X ([14X5.3-3[114X) (which is called by
  [2XSL2IrrepR[102X ([14X5.3-2[114X) when appropriate).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YSecond,  if  [23X\sigma = \lambda - 2[123X for [23Xp = 2[123X, [23X\lambda \geq 2[123X, then the second
  factor of [23XM[123X is isomorphic to [23X\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}[123X, and collapses in [23X2M[123X; we
  call  this case the [13Xextremal[113X family. Here, [23X\operatorname{Aut}(M,Q)[123X is itself
  abelian,  so  we  let [23X\mathfrak{A} = \operatorname{Aut}(M,Q)[123X. This group has
  the following structure:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YFor [23X\lambda = 2[123X, [23Xt=1[123X, [23X\mathfrak{A} = \langle \tau \rangle[123X where [23X\tau :
        (x,y) \mapsto (y,x)[123X; a character [23X\chi[123X is primitive if [23X\chi(\tau) = -1[123X.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YFor  [23X\lambda  =  2[123X,  [23Xt  =  3[123X,  [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X  is  trivial; there are no
        primitive characters.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YFor [23X\lambda = 3[123X or [23X4[123X, [23X\mathfrak{A} = \{\pm 1\}[123X; there are no primitive
        characters.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YFinally,  for  [23X\lambda  \geq  5[123X, [23X\mathfrak{A} = \langle \alpha \rangle
        \times  \langle  -1  \rangle[123X  with  [23X\alpha  \neq  -1[123X of order 2, and a
        character [23X\chi \in \widehat{\mathfrak{A}}[123X is primitive if [23X\chi(\alpha)
        = -1[123X. Note that, for this special case, the usual test for primitivity
        (described  in  Section  [14X2.1[114X)  fails,  as  there  are  no  elements of
        [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X fixing [23X2M[123X pointwise.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YOutside of the above two special cases, we define[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\mathfrak{A}       =       \{\varepsilon       \in       M^\times       \mid
  \operatorname{Nm}(\varepsilon) = 1 \}[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0Yacting  on [23XM[123X by multiplication; see [NW76, Section 2.3 - 2.4]. The structure
  of  [23X\mathfrak{A}[123X  is  as  follows,  and  a  character  is primitive if it is
  injective  on  [23X\langle\omega\rangle  \leq \mathfrak{A}[123X, with [23X\omega = \alpha[123X
  except when specified:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YSuppose  [23Xp  \neq  2[123X,  [23X\lambda \geq 2[123X, [23X\sigma \in \{1, \dots, \lambda -
        1\}[123X.   If   [23X\lambda   \geq  3[123X  and  [23X\sigma=t=1[123X,  then  [23X\mathfrak{A}  =
        \langle\alpha\rangle  \times  \langle\beta\rangle[123X with [23X\alpha[123X of order
        [23X3^{\lambda-2}[123X   and  [23X\beta[123X  of  order  6.  Otherwise,  [23X\mathfrak{A}  =
        \langle\alpha\rangle  \times  \langle-1\rangle[123X  with  [23X\alpha[123X  of order
        [23Xp^{\lambda - \sigma}[123X.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YSuppose  [23Xp=2[123X,  [23X\lambda  \geq  3[123X, [23X\sigma \in \{0, \dots, \lambda - 3\}[123X.
        Then  the  structure  of  [23X(M,Q)[123X)  depends  on  [23Xr,t[123X,  but only up to an
        equivalence class described in [Nob76, Satz 4]; we assume without loss
        of   generality   that   [23Xr,t[123X   are  minimal  positive  representatives
        thereunder. Then:[133X
  
        [30X    [33X[0;12YIf  [23X\sigma  =  0[123X,  [23Xr  \in  \{1,3\}[123X,  [23Xt=1[123X,  and [23X\lambda = 3[123X, then
              [23X\mathfrak{A}  =  \langle\beta\rangle[123X with [23X\beta = (0,1)[123X of order
              4. Here [23X\omega = -1 = \beta^2[123X.[133X
  
        [30X    [33X[0;12YIf  [23X\sigma  =  0[123X,  [23Xr  \in \{1,3\}[123X, [23Xt=1[123X, and [23X\lambda \geq 4[123X, then
              [23X\mathfrak{A}  =  \langle\alpha\rangle \times \langle\beta\rangle[123X
              with  [23X\alpha  = (1 \mod{4},4)[123X of order [23X2^{\lambda-3}[123X and [23X\beta =
              (0,1)[123X of order 4.[133X
  
        [30X    [33X[0;12YIf  [23X\sigma  =  0[123X,  [23Xr  \in  \{1,3\}[123X,  [23Xt=5[123X,  and [23X\lambda = 3[123X, then
              [23X\mathfrak{A}  =  \langle\beta\rangle[123X with [23X\beta = (2,1)[123X of order
              4. Here [23X\omega = -1[123X.[133X
  
        [30X    [33X[0;12YIf  [23X\sigma  =  0[123X,  [23Xr  \in \{1,3\}[123X, [23Xt=5[123X, and [23X\lambda \geq 4[123X, then
              [23X\mathfrak{A} = \langle\alpha\rangle \times \langle-1\rangle[123X with
              [23X\alpha  =  (2,3  \mod{4})[123X  of order [23X2^{\lambda-2}[123X. Here [23X\omega =
              -\alpha^2[123X when [23X\lambda = 4[123X and [23X\omega = \alpha[123X otherwise.[133X
  
        [30X    [33X[0;12YIf  [23X\sigma  =  0[123X,  [23Xr  =  1[123X, [23Xt \in \{3,7\}[123X, and [23X\lambda = 3[123X, then
              [23X\mathfrak{A} = \langle-1\rangle[123X. Here [23X\omega = -1[123X.[133X
  
        [30X    [33X[0;12YIf  [23X\sigma  =  0[123X, [23Xr = 1[123X, [23Xt \in \{3,7\}[123X, and [23X\lambda \geq 4[123X, then
              [23X\mathfrak{A} = \langle\alpha\rangle \times \langle-1\rangle[123X with
              [23X\alpha = (1 \mod{4},4)[123X of order [23X2^{\lambda-3}[123X.[133X
  
        [30X    [33X[0;12YIf  [23X\sigma  = 1[123X, then [23X\mathfrak{A} = \langle\alpha\rangle \times
