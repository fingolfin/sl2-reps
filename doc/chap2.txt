  [33X[0;0YThe   group   [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X   is  generated  by  [23X\mathfrak{s}[123X  =
  [10X[[0,1],[-1,0]][110X and [23X\mathfrak{t}[123X = [10X[[1,1],[0,1]][110X (which satisfy the relations
  [23X\mathfrak{s}^4   =  (\mathfrak{st})^3  =  \mathrm{id}[123X).  Thus,  any  complex
  representation  [23X\rho  :  \mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) \to \mathbb{C}^n[123X (where [23Xn
  \in  \mathbb{Z}^+[123X is called the [13Xdegree[113X of [23X\rho[123X) is defined by the [23Xn \times n[123X
  matrices  [23XS  =  \rho(\mathfrak{s})[123X  and [23XT = \rho(\mathfrak{t})[123X. In fact, any
  such representation factors through [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/\ell\mathbb{Z})[123X
  for  some  [23X\ell \in \mathbb{Z}^+[123X; the smallest such [23X\ell[123X is called the [13Xlevel[113X
  of [23X\rho[123X. We therefore present representations in the form of a record[133X
  
  [33X[0;0Y[10Xrec(S, T, degree, level, name)[110X[133X
  
  [33X[0;0Ywhere  the  name follows the conventions of [NW76]. Note that our definition
  of  [23X\mathfrak{s}[123X  follows that of [Nob76]; other authors prefer the inverse,
  i.e.  [10X[[0,-1],[1,0]][110X. When working with that convention, one must invert the

  [1X2.1 [33X[0;0YConstruction[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YFor  any  representation [23X\rho[123X of [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X, we may decompose
  [23X\rho[123X  as  a  direct  sum of irreducible representations of prime-power level
  using   the  Chinese  remainder  theorem.  Therefore,  to  characterize  all
  representations   of  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X,  it  suffices  to  consider
  irreducible                        representations                        of
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YSuch  representations  may  be  constructed  using  Weyl  representations as
  described  in  [Nob76,  Section  1].  The  Weyl representations used by this
  package  have  the  following  form. Let [23Xp[123X be a prime number and [23X\lambda \in
  \mathbb{Z}^+[123X.  Choose a [23X\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}[123X-module [23XM[123X of rank 1 or
  2, and a quadratic form [23XQ[123X on [23XM[123X, such that [23X(M,Q)[123X is of one of the three types
  described  in  [14X2.2[114X.  The  [13Xquadratic  module[113X  [23X(M,Q)[123X  then  gives  rise  to  a
  representation   of  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})[123X,  denoted
  [23XW(M,Q)[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWith    a   finite   number   of   exceptions,   every   representation   of
  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z})[123X    may    be    found   as   a
  subrepresentation  of  [23XW(M,Q)[123X  for  an  appropriate  choice  of [23X(M,Q)[123X [NW76,
  Hauptsatz  2].  The  18 exceptions may be found as the tensor product of two
  such  subrepresentations;  these  may  be  generated with [2XSL2Reps_Exceptions[102X
  ([14X3.3-1[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YRepresentations  of  [23X\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})[123X  may  then be found as direct
  sums of these prime-power representations.[133X
  
  
  [1X2.2 [33X[0;0YWeyl representation types[133X[101X
  
  
  [1X2.2-1 [33X[0;0YType D[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet [23Xp[123X be prime and [23X\lambda \geq 1[123X. Then the Weyl representation arising from
  the   quadratic   module  with  [23XM  =  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}  \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}[123X  and [23XQ(x,y) = \frac{xy}{p^\lambda}[123X is said to
  be  of type [23XD[123X and denoted [23XD(p,\lambda)[123X. Information on [23X(M,Q)[123X may be obtained
  via  [2XSL2Reps_ModuleD[102X  ([14X5.1-1[114X),  and  subrepresentations of [23XD(p,\lambda)[123X with
  level [23Xp^\lambda[123X may be constructed via [2XSL2Reps_RepD[102X ([14X5.1-2[114X).[133X
  
  
  [1X2.2-2 [33X[0;0YType N[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet [23Xp[123X be prime and [23X\lambda \geq 1[123X. Then the Weyl representation arising from
  the   quadratic   module  with  [23XM  =  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}  \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}[123X       and       [23XQ(x,y)       =      \frac{x^2
  +xy+\frac{1+u}{4}y^2}{p^\lambda}[123X (where, for [23Xp \neq 2[123X, [23Xu[123X is chosen so that [23Xu
  \equiv  3[123X  mod  4  with [23X\left(\frac{-u}{p}\right) = -1[123X, and for [23Xp=2[123X, [23Xu=3[123X) is
  said  to  be of type [23XN[123X and denoted [23XN(p,\lambda)[123X. Information on [23X(M,Q)[123X may be
  obtained via [2XSL2Reps_ModuleN[102X ([14X5.2-1[114X), and subrepresentations of [23XD(p,\lambda)[123X
  with level [23Xp^\lambda[123X may be constructed via [2XSL2Reps_RepN[102X ([14X5.2-2[114X).[133X
  
  
  [1X2.2-3 [33X[0;0YType R[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe construction of type [23XR[123X varies depending on whether [23Xp=2[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YFirst,  if  [23Xp[123X  is  an  odd prime, let [23X\lambda \geq 2[123X, [23X\sigma \in \{1, \dots,
  \lambda\}[123X,  and  [23Xr,t  \in \{1,u\}[123X with [23Xu[123X a quadratic non-residue mod [23Xp[123X. Then
  define        [23XM        =        \mathbb{Z}/p^\lambda\mathbb{Z}        \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/p^{\lambda-\sigma}\mathbb{Z}[123X  and [23XQ(x,y) = \frac{r(x^2 + p^\sigma
  t y^2)}{p^\lambda}[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YOn  the  other  hand,  if  [23Xp=2[123X,  let  [23X\lambda \geq 2[123X, [23X\sigma \in \{0, \dots,
  \lambda-2\}[123X    and    [23Xr,t    \in    \{1,3,5,7\}[123X.    Then    define    [23XM    =
  \mathbb{Z}/2^{\lambda-1}\mathbb{Z}                                    \oplus
  \mathbb{Z}/2^{\lambda-\sigma-1}\mathbb{Z}[123X   and   [23XQ(x,y)   =  \frac{r(x^2  +
  2^\sigma t y^2)}{2^\lambda}[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  either  case,  the  resulting representation is said to be of type [23XR[123X and
  denoted  [23XR(p,\lambda,\sigma,r,t)[123X.  Information  on [23X(M,Q)[123X may be obtained via
  [2XSL2Reps_ModuleR[102X  ([14X5.3-1[114X),  and subrepresentations of [23XR(p,\lambda,\sigma,r,t)[123X
  with  level [23Xp^\lambda[123X may be constructed via [2XSL2Reps_RepR[102X ([14X5.3-2[114X). Note that
  if  [23X\sigma  =  \lambda[123X  for [23Xp \neq 2[123X, then the second factor of [23XM[123X is trivial
  (and   hence   [23Xt[123X   is   irrelevant);   this   special  case  is  handled  by
  [2XSL2Reps_RepRUnary[102X ([14X5.3-3[114X).[133X
